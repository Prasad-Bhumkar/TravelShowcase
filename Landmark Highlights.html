Featured Landmarks

City Hall

Established in 1890, City Hall is a stunning example of neoclassical architecture and a symbol of the city's rich political heritage. Its grand clock tower has witnessed over a century of history.

Did you know? The clock tower chimes every hour and was meticulously restored in 2015 to preserve its original charm.

Golden Arch Bridge

Connecting the city’s two vibrant districts, the Golden Arch Bridge is famed for its elegant arches and panoramic views. It’s a favorite spot for photographers and romantics alike.

Fun fact: Over 10,000 LED bulbs illuminate the bridge nightly, creating a mesmerizing light show.

St. Mary's Cathedral

A breathtaking example of gothic architecture, this cathedral has been a spiritual and cultural beacon for over 500 years. Its stained glass windows tell stories of faith and resilience.

Did you know? The cathedral’s bells are rung manually by a dedicated team of bell ringers during special ceremonies.

City Art Museum

Home to a diverse collection of contemporary and classic art, the museum is a vibrant cultural hub. The sculpture garden showcases works from over 20 talented local artists.

Fun fact: The museum hosts monthly artist talks and interactive workshops.

Old Town Market

A lively marketplace where tradition meets vibrancy. Sample authentic street food, browse handcrafted goods, and soak in the lively atmosphere that has thrived since the 17th century.

Did you know? The market is a melting pot of cultures, hosting festivals year-round.

Central Park

The city’s green oasis, perfect for relaxing strolls, picnics, and vibrant outdoor concerts. The park hosts over 100 cultural events annually, making it a lively community hub.

Fun fact: The park’s fountains are choreographed to music during summer evenings.
